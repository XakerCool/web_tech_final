About: Turandot

Turandot is not just a restaurant â€“ it is a real palace. The XVIII century, which passed for Europe under the sign of chinoiserie (worship of China), gave the Moscow restaurateur Andrei Dellos the idea of creating this restaurant, stunning in its beauty and scope. The mansion is decorated in the style of early Baroque, classicism and Renaissance. These are refined paintings, precious porcelain vases, authentic tapestries, stucco, antique clocks and fireplaces. The interior details of the restaurant have been made by two workshops of wood carvers, artists, sculptors for 6 years. Everything here, including the carved chandeliers made by modern masters, is of museum value. The Turandot restaurant has 12 halls, two main and ten banquet halls. The menu is replete with dishes of Japanese, European, fusion and Chinese cuisines. Alan Yao, the only chef who has been awarded two Michelin stars for Asian cuisine, is responsible for the latter. The restaurant has tea sommeliers who advise guests to choose a drink, taking into account physical well-being, mental state, time of day and compatibility with Oriental dishes. In the tea card of the restaurant: "Oolong" with the aroma of milk, "Mountain peaks in a pink haze", "Silver needles of the Junshan Mountains", "Pu-erh" of 10 years of aging and many others. Musical arrangement to match the institution: instrumental music (harp, violin, cello) sounds here every evening.
